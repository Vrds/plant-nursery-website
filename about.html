About Plants

Plants are the green elements whose presence is indispensable for the entire mankind. They are the living organisms that prepare their own food by photosynthesis. They have over 3,00,000 different species, and they majorly include grasses, trees, shrubs and likewise.

Other than having aesthetic value, plants have a lot of health benefits too. Many houseplants absorb hazardous compounds present in man-made materials that are known to "off-gas" pollutants into the air in your home, school, and office, such as formaldehyde, benzene, and trichloroethylene. Plants produce water vapour into the air, increasing humidity, which can aid respiratory and skin health by counteracting the drying effects of heating systems.

Since the entire green family is termed as plants, there are various divisions of plants.

  • Mosses- These are the non-vascular plants. They propagate and grow through spores, rather than seeds or flowers.
  • Ferns - Ferns are vascular plants that neither have seeds nor flowers. They have some special tissue cells through which they receive both water and nutrients.
  • Conifers - These are the cone-bearing seed plants, having vascular tissue. All the living conifers can be categorized into woody plants.
  • Flowering Plants - As per the name, these are the ones that bear flowers. They look extremely attractive and can be placed both indoors and outdoors.
  • Plant - Features and Uses A basic characteristic of plants is to adapt to the environment and grow accordingly. The biggest use of plants is that it provides shelter and food to a number of living organisms, including humans. Not to forget, a number of fruits and flowers have edible and medicinal properties too.
